IDUKKI

Previous Next

A proud home of South India’s highest peak, Anamudi that stands at a height of 2,695 meters, Idukki is one of the most stunning hill stations in Kerala.Housing 13 other peaks with a height of over 2000 meters, it attracts tourist from across the globe for its wildlife sanctuaries, hill stations, spice plantation centers, and mountain treks.

Idukki is known for its scenic beauty and is believed to be a heaven for nature lovers. The climate of Idukki is Tropical. There is significant rainfall in most of the months of the year. The short dry season has little effect on the overall climate. The average annual temperature is 24.4 degrees Celsius in Idukki. The average of Precipitation here is 2226 mm.

Tourists from all over the world visit the place to spend time amidst its beautiful valleys, dense forests, and extensive wildlife. The place can be visited throughout the year except for the rainy season. However, the winter season is considered the best season to explore the beauty of Idukki. Famed for its panoramic beauty and everything green and pretty, Idukki is one of the most frequented districts among all the 14 districts in Kerala. A land of temples, dams, and waterfalls, the Idukki district in Kerala is a perfect spot to rejuvenate and replenish your soul at.


MAJOR THINGS TO DO

  1. Trekking

    All the trekking enthusiasts, Idukki is a paradise for you and is an amazing spot when you can challenge your inner adventurous soul. There are majorly three places in Idukki where you will find picturesque trails for your trek. The Chinnar Valley trek is quite famous among the locals and tourists and you must add it to your list of things to do in Idukki in Kerala.

    Locations: Urumbi Hills, Thangalpara Valley, Ramakkalmedu

  2. Paragliding

    Counted among one of the most popular things to do in Idukki, Tandem Paragliding is a delightful experience that you must indulge in on your vacation. If all you want to do is fly and soak in the scenic view of the lush green forest of Idukki districts then you should straight away head to the hill station of Vagamon in Kerala.

    Timings: 11 Am – 4:30 Pm
    Location: Vagamon, Kerala

  3. Misty Mountain Resort

    An exotic stay at the Misty Mountain Resort is one of those things to do in Idukki that you can’t miss out on! Wooden Chalets, lush green surroundings, top-notch services, and scrumptious local food is what your stay will be all about.

    Apt for: Nature lovers, peace seekers, photography enthusiasts.
    Location: Peermade, Kuttikkanam – 685 531, Idukki Dist. Kerala

  4. Idukki Wildlife Sanctuary

    Spread across an area of 70 sq. kms, the Idukki Wildlife Sanctuary is one of the famous tourist places in Idukki. Situated at an altitude of 450 to 748 meters, the sanctuary occupies the forest land between the Cheruthoni and Periyar rivers, and is a natural habitat for several species of animals. Featuring a picturesque lake around the sanctuary, the whole region is covered by tropical, and evergreen trees. This is one of the most popular things to do in Idukki.

    Apt for: Nature lovers, wild-life enthusiasts.
    Location: Vellappara, Painavu, Idukki, Kerala 685603
